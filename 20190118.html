Intellectual property

An attempt to remedy a failure in the market for knowledge

What is a market failure?

Four types of goods

Excludable Non-excludable
Rivalrous Private goods (computers, cars) Common-pool goods (air)
Non-rivalrous Club goods (wifi, pay-per-view) Public goods (national defense, information)

Information is a public good

Market failure

Overproduction or underproduction

Two problems

  • Free rider problem
  • Tragedy of the commons

Free rider problem

It costs more to create information than to consume

Tragedy of the commons

Secrets are more valuable personally, but shared knowledge is more valuable to society.

Intellectual property makes public goods private

Open source

a legal construct for cooperation in intellectual property

Shared ownership

Each contributor owns the code she contributes

Involvement

Each contributor has a say in the direction of the project

Why need license?

If you don't license it, other people cannot use your code.

Copyright restrictions apply as soon as someone creates a tangible work.
